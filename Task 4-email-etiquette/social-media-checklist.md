# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Moksh Patel | 25MIM10028**

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do |
|---|----|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear photo, your real name, and accurate information on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub. |
| 2 | **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings** – Review and update who can see your posts, location, and personal details on every platform. |
| 4 | **Engage constructively** – Comment, share, and respond to others in a respectful and thoughtful manner. |
| 5 | **Credit others' work** – Always mention the original creator when sharing someone else's content, image, or idea. |
| 6 | **Report harmful content** – Use platform reporting tools to flag cyberbullying, hate speech, or inappropriate content. |
| 7 | **Keep personal and professional accounts separate** – Use your college email for academic platforms and keep personal social media private. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Don't |
|---|-------|
| 1 | **Don't share personal information publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, or daily routine on public profiles. |
| 2 | **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** – Do not bully others online, and do not stay silent if you witness it happening to someone. |
| 3 | **Don't post in anger or frustration** – Impulsive posts about peers, professors, or institutions can have serious long-term consequences. |
| 4 | **Don't click on unverified links** – Phishing links are often shared via social media DMs, even by people you know whose accounts are compromised. |
| 5 | **Don't overshare location information** – Avoid posting real-time location updates or tagging your exact location in posts. |
| 6 | **Don't plagiarise or repost without permission** – Sharing copyrighted content without credit can lead to account suspension or legal issues. |
| 7 | **Don't use social media during exams or assessments** – Sharing exam questions or answers online is considered academic misconduct. |
