# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

## Tool Used
**Canva** (https://canva.com)

## What the Infographic Shows

The infographic is titled **"Be Digitally Smart!"** and is designed as a one-page visual guide for first-year B.Tech students. It is divided into five colour-coded sections:

1. **What is Digital Literacy?** – Defined as the ability to use digital tools effectively, safely, and responsibly.
2. **Safe Internet Practices** – Tips like using strong passwords, avoiding public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks, and not clicking unknown links.
3. **Professional Online Presence** – Why platforms like GitHub and LinkedIn matter early in your career.
4. **Email Etiquette** – Key points: use a clear subject line, professional greeting, and proper sign-off.
5. **Useful Digital Tools for Students** – Highlights tools like Google Workspace, Canva, HackerRank, and Notion.

## Reflection (150–200 words)

For this task, I used **Canva** to design a digital literacy awareness infographic. Canva is a free, browser-based design tool that offers hundreds of templates, icons, and fonts — making it ideal for students with no graphic design experience.

My infographic covers five key areas of digital literacy: its definition, safe internet practices, professional online presence, email etiquette, and useful student tools. I organised the information into colour-coded sections to make it visually clear and easy to scan quickly, especially for students who might only glance at it briefly.

The most interesting part of this task was realising how much information needs to be filtered when designing for a general audience. A common mistake in infographics is including too much text, which defeats the purpose of a visual medium. I had to rewrite my points multiple times to make them shorter and more impactful.

The most difficult aspect was choosing a colour scheme that looked professional yet approachable for students. I finally settled on a clean blue-and-white theme with orange accents.
