## What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the use of digital platforms — social media, messaging apps, online gaming, or email — to repeatedly harass, humiliate, threaten, or harm another individual. Unlike traditional bullying, cyberbullying can occur 24 hours a day, reaches the victim even in the safety of their own home, and can spread to thousands of people instantly.

---

## Case Study: The Anonymous Group Chat (Realistic Fictional Scenario)

Riya, an 18-year-old first-year engineering student, joined a WhatsApp group created by her classmates for sharing study notes. Within weeks, a subset of the group began making fun of her accent and rural background in indirect messages. One student created an anonymous Instagram account and posted edited screenshots of Riya's profile photos with mocking captions. The posts were shared across multiple class groups and received hundreds of reactions before Riya was even aware of them.

**Step-by-step progression:**
1. Initial teasing in private chats — dismissed as "jokes."
2. Escalation to public platforms (Instagram, Twitter/X) using anonymous accounts.
3. Edited images and fabricated quotes attributed to Riya spread virally.
4. Riya began receiving threatening and humiliating DMs from accounts she did not know.
5. She withdrew socially, stopped attending classes, and her academic performance declined sharply.

**Who is typically targeted:** Students who are new to a group, those from different socioeconomic or regional backgrounds, individuals who speak up against the majority, or those with high visibility on social media.

**Consequences:** Severe anxiety, depression, academic failure, social isolation. In extreme cases documented globally, cyberbullying has led to self-harm and suicide among young people. The perpetrators can face legal consequences under the IT Act, 2000 and IPC sections related to harassment and defamation.

**Source:** Scenario based on documented patterns in UNICEF's 2021 report on cyberbullying in South Asia and the National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) cybercrime data for India.
