## Cyberbullying & Online Safety – 10 Actionable Tips for College Students in India

| # | Tip | Why It Matters |
|---|-----|---------------|
| 1 | **Set all social media accounts to private** and only accept requests from people you know in real life. | Limits who can see and interact with your content. |
| 2 | **Never share personal photos or videos** in groups or with people you have not met in person. | Prevents misuse, morphing, or non-consensual sharing of images. |
| 3 | **Screenshot and save evidence** of any cyberbullying messages, posts, or accounts before blocking or reporting. | Evidence is required when filing a complaint. |
| 4 | **Report the account** using the platform's built-in reporting tool (Instagram, WhatsApp, YouTube all have this) and request removal of harmful content. | Platforms are legally required to act on valid reports. |
| 5 | **Block the bully immediately** — you do not owe anyone a response or explanation online. | Stops further contact and protects your mental health. |
| 6 | **Talk to a trusted adult, counsellor, or friend** if you or someone you know is being bullied online. | Early support prevents escalation and mental health impact. |
| 7 | **Do not retaliate online** — responding in anger can worsen the situation and may be used against you. | Retaliatory messages can be taken out of context. |
| 8 | **Review and audit your digital footprint** — search your own name periodically to see what is publicly visible about you. | Helps you identify and remove content you did not consent to share. |

## 💳 Financial & UPI Safety Tips (Mandatory)

| # | Tip |
|---|-----|
| 9 | **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank account number** with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank or NPCI. Legitimate organisations never ask for this. |
| 10 | **Do not scan QR codes sent by strangers** to "receive" money — scanning a QR code can only send money FROM your account, never to it. |

## 🚨 Reporting Channels

| Resource | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | https://cybercrime.gov.in — Report cyberbullying, financial fraud, image-based abuse, and all cybercrimes online. |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — Available 24x7. Call immediately after a cybercrime incident to freeze fraudulent transactions or report harassment. |
| **CHILDLINE (for minors)** | 1098 — If the victim is under 18, CHILDLINE can provide additional support. |
